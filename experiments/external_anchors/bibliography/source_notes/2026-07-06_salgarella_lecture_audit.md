# Salgarella ILARA lecture — transcript audit (2026-07-06)

Source: E. Salgarella, "The 'Minoan' Linear A script of Bronze Age Crete: is decipherment the only
goal?" (ILARA / Collège de France, 20 Dec 2025). Transcript `Linear_A_Salgarella.srt` (57 kB, ~6.5k
words) supplied by the user; frames OCR'd separately (URLs below). Summary of findings relevant to
logos — quotes are short paraphrase.

## 1. Methodological alignment (strongly corroborates logos)
- **"Is decipherment the only goal?"** — her thesis is that *meaning* is recoverable **without**
  phonetic decipherment via the **context-based combinatorial (internal/textual) method**, which she
  calls "more promising than the etymological method" and which "allows to deduce the approximate
  meaning of words without necessarily knowing the underlying language." → exactly the logos
  schema-induction stance; her flagship example is **KU-RO = total** (our reconciliation anchor).
- She names the **etymological trap** explicitly: preferential readings/restorations make Linear A
  "a good fit for several even unrelated target languages" — independent articulation of the logos
  "English-is-Semitic" enemy and the E8 equal-budget discipline.

## 2. Language verdict (matches the logos null)
- Minoan = **isolate, indigenous to Crete**, "cannot be uncontroversially related to any linguistic
  family" — proposals (Semitic, Anatolian, IE, Greek, Hurrian, Etruscan, Hattic) all unconfirmed;
  "not yet enough data … incontrovertibly to identify the affiliation." **Agglutinative** (high affix
  use). Davis 2013: **VSO** word order from the libation formula.

## 3. ⭐ Toponym/name persistence LA→LB — a cleaner anchor channel than the Egyptian one
She lists **internal Linear A → Linear B toponym correspondences** (place-names surviving the
Minoan→Mycenaean shift, near-identical spelling):
`di-ki-ta` (Mt Dikte) ~ LB di-ka-ta · `pa-i-to` (Phaistos) ~ LB pa-i-to · `tu-ru-sa` (Tylissos) ~ LB
tu-ri-so · `i-da` (Mt Ida) ~ LB i-da-i-jo · `se-to-i-ja` ~ LB se-to-i-ja. Also personal names
`di-de-ru`~LB di-de-ro, `pa-je-re`~LB pa-je-ro (Greek -os added); transaction terms KU-RO=total,
wool→Gk mallos, fig NI/AB30 acrophonic for *nikuleon* (Neumann 1958/62).

**Design implication (important):** the toponym-anchor test could use **LA↔LB internal persistence**
(mediated by the *A-B homomorphy*, which is internal and comparatively well-established) **instead of
/ in addition to** the Egyptian-transcription channel. That removes the whole Egyptian-distortion
model (the dominant false-positive driver in the power pilot) and its calibration dependency — likely
a materially higher-power, lower-risk channel. **Candidate redirect for the next design pass.**

## 4. Resources (actionable data sources)
- **SigLA — "the Sign of Linear A" palaeographical Database** (Salgarella & S. Castellan): open,
  interactive, palaeographic + epigraphic + **statistical** analysis of LA signs & inscriptions, with
  interactive drawings. `https://www.inscribercproject.com/SigLA/` (slide's `…/SigLA/index.htm` is a
  stale path). **A machine-readable, authoritative LA sign+inscription DB with palaeographic variants**
  — directly addresses the transcription-uncertainty gap (Task G) and could augment/replace the
  ingested corpus. → check for a data export/API. **Highest-value follow-up.**
- **LIFE** (Linear A Inscription Finder) — Salgarella, interactive catalogue, under construction.
- **GORILA** (Godart & Olivier, print) — the corpus behind logos silver; **NEW: 1st supplement by
  Del Freo & Zurbach (2025)** = new inscriptions (corpus update to ingest).
- **Younger, "Linear A Texts … phonetic transcription"** — U-Kansas site taken down (2024 retirement);
  now on **Younger's academia.edu as downloadable PDFs** (the logos bronze younger_lineara source).
- **paito project** `https://www.paitoproject.it/en/pa-i-to-project-2` — Phaistos; open-access **3D +
  RTI** models of LA / Cretan-hieroglyphic inscriptions (palaeography).
- **CEFAEL** `https://cefael.efa.gr/…` — EFA digital library (GORILA scans online).
- Ferrara, Montecchi & Valério 2022 — LA sign classification.

## 5. Sign repertory (relevant to inventory + slot features)
GORILA: ~200 simple + ~200 composite + ~50 "measured" (metrogram/fraction) signs. Salgarella's
re-analysis: a **shared CORE of simple signs** + **site-specific local subsets** (mostly hapaxes);
**composite signs are overwhelmingly site-specific**; composition *configurations* are cross-site.
→ corroborates the logos inventory tiers AND the slot-classifier's use of **site-count** as a feature
(site-specificity distinguishes core vocabulary from local names — exactly the TOPONYM_LIKE vs
PERSON_NAME_LIKE recurrence heuristic).
