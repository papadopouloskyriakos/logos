## Power is a step function in orthographic drift
| implanted continuity | recovery | detected | power |
|---|---|---|---|
| **exact** (drift 0) | 100% at n=1 | ✅ | **FULL** — min detectable = **1 pair** |
| genuine drift 1 (unflagged spelling change) | 0% (A1, A2, A3) | ❌ | **ZERO** |
| genuine drift 2 | 0% | ❌ | ZERO |
| transcription-uncertain 1 (damage/composite **flagged**) | recovered by A3 | ✅ | recoverable |

The critical distinction: A3's ≤1-wildcard tolerates **transcription uncertainty** (a flagged
damage/composite position), **not** a genuine orthographic **drift** (a real spelling change such as
LA `tu-ru-sa` → LB `tu-ri-so`). A genuinely drifted true pair is **unrecoverable** without the
forbidden free mapping search.

## Reading
The design has **full power for EXACT administrative continuity** (any single exact pair would be
detected at p ≈ 0.0125) and returned a **clean 0**. It has **zero power for genuinely drifted
continuity**, which is the realistic majority case — recovering it would require the free mapping
search that the primary design forbids. The channel therefore cannot evaluate the drift-inclusive
continuity hypothesis; it can only report — and does — that **no *exact* administrative toponym
continuity exists above the null.**
