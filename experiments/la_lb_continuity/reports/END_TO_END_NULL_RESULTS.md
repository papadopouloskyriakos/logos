## Reading
- **The channel is specific.** Even with 8/11 PRIMARY candidates only 2 signs long, the
  frequency-matched chance-match rate is ~1.25% per family and ~3.2% across *everything* tried. Expected
  spurious matches ≈ 0.01. A single observed match would have been meaningful (p ≈ 0.01–0.03).
- **Real-data nulls are clean:** non-toponym LA controls and false LB targets produce **exactly 0**
  matches — the pipeline does not manufacture matches from non-toponyms.
- **Site/genre are not load-bearing** in the primary exact-identity match, so their shuffles are
  degenerate (null ≡ observed). The result does not depend on them.
- **Observed = 0** sits squarely inside every null; there is **no administrative continuity signal**,
  and the absence is not attributable to poor specificity.

## What the nulls do NOT resolve
Specificity is good, but the nulls say nothing about **sensitivity to realistically *drifted*
continuity** (e.g. LA `tu-ru-sa` ↔ LB `tu-ri-so`, a 2-position orthographic change across the
Minoan→Mycenaean transition). Exact/near-exact matching (A1/A2/A3≤1-wildcard) cannot span 2-position
drift without the forbidden free mapping search. That sensitivity question is the subject of §XIII
(power) and is the likely determinant of the readiness verdict.
