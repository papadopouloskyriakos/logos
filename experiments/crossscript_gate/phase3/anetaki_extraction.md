Source: `corpus/bronze/kanta_etal_2024_anetaki/` (SHA-256 `87dad27b…57d2`; CC BY-NC-SA 4.0;
Ariadne Suppl. 5, pp. 27–43). Rule honored: **nothing was read off photographs or drawings** —
every item below is printed by the editors, page-cited. GORILA sign numbering per the

## The inscribed objects

**KN Zg 57 — the ivory Ring** (elephant-tusk 'slice', outer ⌀ c. 13.5–14 cm; faces A top /
B bottom / C external / D internal; found with its handle in the Ivory Repository, NW corner
of Neopalatial Room 1, pp. 30–32, 34). **KN Zg 58 — the ivory handle** (quadrangular section,
c. 13 cm; faces α β γ δ; pp. 40–41). Provisionally "a religious scepter" (Kanta, p. 32).

## What the editors PRINT (exhaustively)

### Ring, Face A (pp. 36–38)
- Left half: 12 quadruped-animal *metopes* (iconography deferred to the final publication,
  fn. 8 "Kanta forthcoming"); all animals face left.
- Right half: "16 whole or partially preserved signs, each in a *metope*… At least 18 signs,
  therefore, were originally present" (p. 37). Direction indicator: "the 'rhyton' (Linear A
  sign **A 664**), which in its only attestation in Linear A on tablet **PH 8a.3**, is
  oriented facing to the right" (p. 37).
- Palaeographic comparanda to the silver pin **KN Zf 31**: "the signs **AB 60 (RA), AB 28 (I),
  AB 24 (NE)** and **AB 09 (SE)**" (p. 37).
- "at least ten vases are engraved… Some vases have a syllabic sign, placed above in ligature…
  (**VAS+PA, VAS+RU**, etc.)… the signs for the rhyton and the tripod are the two vase signs on
  the Ring that do not have an associated syllabogram" (p. 38); "Six vases recorded on the Ring
  share an identical typology, that of an amphora" (p. 38).
- NO phonetic sign GROUPS on Face A (p. 39: "we have no such phonetic sign groups on Face A").

### Ring, Face B (p. 39)
- "groups of signs, but also single syllabograms and logograms… many *lacunae*"; direction
  left-to-right "judging by the orientation of **AB 81 (KU), AB 40 (WI)** and **AB 60 (RA)**".
- "**six groups of signs that are well-preserved and perfectly recognizable**, followed by
  three ideograms/logograms… in Linear B [they] have the value **GRA, probably FAR, and
  OLIV**. Each occupies its own *metope*… no accompanying numbers or fractional signs."
- **The six sign groups are NOT transliterated anywhere in the article.**

### Ring, Face C (pp. 39–40)
- "At least 40% of Face C has extensive surface damage… We can recognize **at least nine
  groups of signs and five logograms** for different types of textiles differing from each
  other by the number of fringes. One textile sign is ligatured with the sign **AB 77 (KA)**
  inside it" (pp. 39–40). Direction left-to-right from "**AB 60 (RA), AB 01 (TA), AB 81
  (KU)**, etc." (p. 40 — printed EXACTLY thus; note: GORILA/standard AB 01 is *da*; the
  article prints "AB 01 (TA)". Recorded verbatim, not resolved here.)
- **None of the nine groups is transliterated.**

### Ring, Face D (p. 40)
- "extremely damaged… almost exclusively a long sequence of about nine signs for animal hide,
  none entirely preserved, each in a *metope*… a trace of a sign that seems to be ligatured
  with the hide ideogram (**AB 180**). The ligatured sign can be restored as the upper curved
  portion of syllabogram **AB 77 (KA), AB 78 (QE), or AB 70 (KO)**." Hide-ideogram parallel:
  Phaistos Linear A tablets.

### Ring totals (p. 40)
- "approximately **84 signs completely or partially preserved, and 35 in small traces or
  probably present**… The total number then is about **119 signs**… **the longest known
  Linear A inscription**" (vs HT 123's 105, of which >40% are numerals/separators, leaving
  ~60). "our inscription contains **no numerals at all** on any of its faces… few parallels of
  the sign-groups on the Ring with sign-groups on extant Linear A texts" (p. 40).
- Repeated signs on the Ring: "**AB 08 (A), AB 81 (KU), AB 07 (DI) and AB 77 (KA)**" — incised
  "always in the same way and in a very calligraphic manner" (p. 41). An **AB 08 + AB 77
  (A+KA) ligature** appears on BOTH Ring and handle, stacked oppositely (Ring: A above KA;
  handle: A below KA) — evidence of "two different hands" (p. 41).

### Handle KN Zg 58 (pp. 40–41) — "an accounting text… numerical and fractional signs"
- **Face α:** "two probable vases, each ligatured with a phonetic sign… followed by a sign for
  ten. After these appears a **probable new sign in the Linear A sign repertory**. This sign is
  followed by two signs attested in the Hieroglyphic script: ***180** and ***181**" (p. 41).
- **Face β:** "at the right end, a **sequence of four signs** is recorded. Only the first sign
  is doubtful, because it is very damaged. On the left half… likely a sign for an animal hide"
  (p. 41). **The four signs are NOT transliterated.**
- **Face γ:** "signs for a boar and probably a pig… without numerals" (p. 41).
- **Face δ:** "two vases, with a sign above in ligature, are followed by units, and also by
  fractions… **Six different signs for fractions are recorded in sequence**… a different
  sequence of values than those suggested until now" (pp. 41–42; fn. 12 cf. Corazza et al.
  2021).

### Other Anetaki inscriptions in this article
The article's "some inscriptions" resolves to **KN Zg 57 and KN Zg 58 only** (the epigraphic
section, pp. 34–42, covers exactly these two carriers, treated as separate documents, p. 40).
No other inscribed object receives epigraphic treatment here.

## What remains UNPUBLISHED (the §2 statement)

**No transliterated sign sequence of any length is printed anywhere in the article.** All
sign-group content — the six "perfectly recognizable" Face-B groups, the ≥9 Face-C groups, the
Face-β four-sign sequence, the Face-α ligature readings, the fraction-sign sequence and its
six signs, and every Face-A syllabogram beyond the comparanda list — is deferred to the final
publication: **Kanta (ed.), forthcoming, *Anetaki II: the Neopalatial Room 1 and the Ivory
Repository*** (fns. 6, 8, 10, 13). Those stretches are recorded here as `unpublished`; reading
them from the article's photographs (Figs. 6–7) is out of scope and was not done.
