## Objective
Audit primary/authoritative external sources for **genuinely NEW, underused MULTI-SLOT anchors** — not
more copies of the toponym lineage already in the 115-record inventory. A candidate graduates to a
*new independent anchor* only if it clears **all four** pre-set bars:

| bar | threshold |
|---|---|
| constrained slots | ≥ 4 |
| independent source lineages (Art. XI dependency-distinct) | ≥ 2 |
| LA contexts / sites | ≥ 2 |
| prediction outside the derivation form | ≥ 1 |

Counts are generated by `scripts/d_source_expansion.py` over `corpus/silver/inscriptions_structured.json`
(1,341 inscriptions, 1,165 distinct word-forms). GORILA/LB values label benchmark toponyms only — never a
model input (Art. XII non-circularity).

## Sources audited (register in `data/source_expansion/new_anchors.json → external_source_register`)

1. **Egyptian — Amenhotep III "Aegean List", Kom el-Hetan** (statue base, mortuary temple; Edel 1966,
   *Die Ortsnamenlisten aus dem Totentempel Amenophis III.*; re-collated Edel & Görg 2005; discussion
   Cline 2011 *JAEI*; Kelder 2010). The single richest *independent* (non-LA, non-LB) source of Aegean
   toponyms. Egyptian group-writing renderings (Edel): Amnisos `i-m-n-y-s3` (im[nš]); Phaistos
   `b3-y-š3-ti` (byšt); Kydonia `k3-tw-n3-y` (ktny); Knossos `k-n-š` (Kenesch); Lyktos `r-k-t` (Leket).
   **Dependency cluster:** EGYPTIAN_EPIGRAPHIC — distinct from LA↔LB continuity and from Cypriot/Salgarella.
2. **Egyptian — BM EA 5647 writing-board ("Keftiu names")** (Peet 1927; Duhoux 2003; 2024 reassessment).
   ~7–9 Egyptian renderings of Cretan/Aegean *personal* names.
3. **Egyptian Semitic-loan phonology — Hoch 1994** (*Semitic Words in Egyptian Texts of the NK & TIP*,
   Princeton). Read for the group-writing→sound model underpinning the EA-13 "frozen" correspondence.
   It is a *phonological calibration*, **not** a source of Aegean anchors: its ~500 words are NW-Semitic,
   not Aegean/Cretan.
4. **Near-Eastern ethnonym — Akkadian/Ugaritic Caphtor/Kaptara** (`kptr`, Mari ARM, Ugaritic KTU).
   Single-morpheme ethnonym for Crete; no multi-slot toponym, no LA-attested lexeme.
5. **LA↔LB toponym continuity** (Steele & Meissner 2017; GORILA) — the *existing* lineage; re-measured
   here as the comparison baseline, contributes no new source.

Also considered and rejected as anchor sources (no securely-matched, dependency-distinct LA attestation):
Hittite/Luwian/Hurrian external names; Cretan/Aegean toponyms beyond the five above; admin titles
(KU-RO/KI-RO are internal-structural, already durable L2/L3); seal-owner names (no bilingual seal);
commodity/measure loan-etymologies (KU-NI-SU "emmer" ~ Sem. *kunīšu*, SA-SA-RA-ME, sesame ~ *šumšum*) —
these are **value-blind semantic guesses that presuppose the LA reading** (circular, Art. XII), each
resting on a single etymological proposal (one lineage), with no out-of-derivation prediction.

## Measured attestation — the decisive intersection

**Kom el-Hetan Cretan toponyms vs. Linear A** (`kom_el_hetan_toponym_table`):

| toponym | Egyptian rendering | eg. slots | LA-attested as this toponym? | LA slots | LA sites |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Amnisos | i-m-n-y-s3 | 4 | **no** | — | — |
| Phaistos | b3-y-š3-ti | 4 | **yes** (PA-I-TO) | 3 | 1 (Haghia Triada) |
| Kydonia | k3-tw-n3-y | 3 | **no** | — | — |
| Knossos | k-n-š | 3 | **no** (KU-NI-SU = a commodity, not the city) | — | — |
| Lyktos | r-k-t | 3 | **no** | — | — |

Only **Phaistos / PA-I-TO** appears in *both* the independent Egyptian record *and* Linear A — and it is
**3 slots at 1 site**. Amnisos, Kydonia, Knossos, Lyktos are attested in **Linear B only**, never securely
in Linear A. (The `A-MI…`, `KU-NI…`, `RI-KA-TA`, `*309C-RU-KA` hits in the corpus are unrelated lexemes,
not toponym matches — see `la_probe_hits` in the data.)

**The LA multi-slot toponyms that DO reach ≥4 slots come only from the LA↔LB lineage and are absent from
every independent external source** (`la_lb_continuity_toponym_table`):
SE-TO-I-JA (4 slots, 1 site), SU-KI-RI-TA (4, 1), A-TU-RI-SI-TI/TU-RU-SA = Tylissos (5, **2 sites**),
A-DI-KI-TE = Dikte (4, 1). None is in the Egyptian record → each has **exactly one** value-bearing lineage
(LB transfer), so none clears the ≥2-independent-lineage bar. (Tylissos alone clears ≥4 slots + ≥2 sites but
fails ≥2 lineages.)

**LA multi-slot (≥4-sign) forms attested at ≥2 sites** are 7 in total, and every cross-site one is a
**religious/libation-formula word with no external source lineage at all** — A-TA-I-\*301-WA-JA (5 sites),
JA-SA-SA-RA-ME (5), I-PI-NA-MA (5), U-NA-KA-NA-SI (3), SA-SA-RA-ME (2), A-RI-NI-TA (2), MA-KA-I-TA (2).
They have the best cross-site spread in the corpus but are **unreadable and unanchorable**: no external
edition supplies a value, so they cannot become anchors (they are the durable internal L2/L3 libation
constraint, not an external lexical anchor).

## Grading against the four bars

| candidate | ≥4 slots | ≥2 indep. lineages | ≥2 LA sites | ≥1 out-of-deriv. prediction | graduates? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Phaistos (PA-I-TO) | ✗ (3) | **✓** (LA↔LB **+** Egyptian byšt) | ✗ (1) | ✗ | **no** |
| Tylissos (A-TU-RI-SI-TI) | ✓ (5) | ✗ (LA↔LB only) | ✓ (2) | ✗ | **no** |
| Setoija / Sybrita / Dikte | ✓ (4) | ✗ (LA↔LB only) | ✗ (1) | ✗ | **no** |
| Amnisos/Kydonia/Knossos/Lyktos | (eg. only) | — | **0 LA attestations** | — | **no** |
| BM EA 5647 personal names | (varies) | ✗ (no matched LA name) | 0 | ✗ | **no** |
| commodity loan-etymologies | varies | ✗ (1, circular) | some | ✗ | **no** |

**No candidate clears all four bars.** Phaistos is the *only* anchor with two genuinely independent source
lineages, but it is sub-threshold on slots (3) and sites (1). Critically, even Phaistos's second lineage is
**not value-bearing independently**: the Egyptian `byšt` fixes no LA *sign* value without first assuming the
LA reading of PA-I-TO — so under Art. XI it corroborates the *identification* but earns no independent
absolute-value channel (SEED_A stays 0).

## Verdict (mechanical)

**`SOURCE_EXPANSION_NULL`** — `n_new_independent_anchors = 0`.

No new INDEPENDENT multi-slot anchor exists in the audited primary sources. This is not a quota failure; it
is a structural fact of the record: the set of Aegean toponyms attested in an independent external source
(Egyptian) and the set attested in Linear A at ≥4 slots are **disjoint**, their only overlap (Phaistos) being
short and single-site. Per campaign policy, WP-D records the null and the campaign continues; the frozen
paper is unaffected (this is post-submission child-branch research, Art. XVII append-only).

## Assumptions logged (Art. XVIII)
- **A-D1:** Edel 1966 toponym identifications (byšt=Phaistos etc.) are accepted as the standard reading;
  they are contested in detail (Kelder, Cline) but the *set* of Cretan names is stable. Sensitivity: none of
  the disputed alternatives adds an LA-attested ≥4-slot overlap.
- **A-D2:** "LA-attested as toponym" requires the securely-expected sign sequence, not a substring match;
  substring hits are reported but not counted (guards against the multiple-testing / cherry-pick trap, Inv. 8).
- **A-D3:** Egyptian and LA attestations of the *same* toponym are physically independent, but their linkage
  shares the modern toponym identification → corroborates identification, does **not** earn an independent
  absolute-value lineage (Art. XI/XII).
