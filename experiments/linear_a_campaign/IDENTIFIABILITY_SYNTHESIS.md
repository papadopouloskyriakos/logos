A phonetic reading needs one value per distinct sign (**259 parameters**). The corpus supplies **fewer than
212** independent multi-sign, non-dominant-site constraints — and the *true* figure is far lower once the
83.8% hapax non-recurrence (no cross-validation) and the single-lineage label circularity are applied. This
is the same one-deep / LOTO-fragile signature that produced every individual negative: **the information is
below the identifiability floor.** Unicity is not the bottleneck — *independent constraint mass* is.

## What would change the verdict (a boundary, not a wall)

The ceiling is liftable only from **outside** the present corpus. Concretely, any ONE of:
1. **Substantially more excavated multi-sign LA inscriptions** — raising syntagmatic mass above the hapax
   floor (the record is currently fully indexed; only pending editiones principes — Kommos, Hagia Photini,
   ZA 11+13 joins — or new excavation can move it).
2. **A genuinely independent external anchor set** — more securely-identified Cretan toponyms in the Egyptian
   (or other) record with ≥4 distinctive skeleton slots and a correspondence not resting on one training
   record. The frozen power pass shows the *design* would then be powered.
3. **A bilingual / biscriptal** — none exists.

None is conjurable by any protocol redesign. LOGOS does **not** conclude that Linear A is inherently or
permanently undecipherable (forbidden, Preamble) — only that **it is underdetermined by the present corpus and
sources**, with the above as the specific unlock conditions.
