## Scope correction (do not over-describe it)

This is a Cambridge **Element** — a short-form peer-reviewed monograph, **~60 pp** of text + refs at
p. 61 — **not** a full-length "definitive synthesis book." Mis-citing its scope/length would itself
draw reviewer correction. It is, however, the **single most current (Aug 2025) authoritative
one-author overview of Linear A**, by Ester Salgarella (Aarhus IAS), author of the SigLA database
(already a logos data dependency) and the 2020 A/B-relationship monograph. **Failing to engage it is
a desk-reject risk for any 2026 Linear-A submission** — it directly covers reading (§7) and language
(§8), logos's two substantive surfaces.

Its stance supports logos's honest framing: the abstract states **"Linear A is still undeciphered,
and the language it encodes ('Minoan') thus remains elusive"** — Salgarella claims no decipherment.
