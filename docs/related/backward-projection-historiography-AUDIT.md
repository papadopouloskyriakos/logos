## Corrections that matter

Every PARTIALLY/CONTRADICTED verdict with its correction, most consequential first. Items marked **[re-run]** were added or updated from the 2026-07-03 web-history/web-recent re-run.

1. **Misattributed quote — "at least 10 or 11 signs… palaeographical" (CONTRADICTED in sm2017-pdf; PARTIALLY in web-companion; PARTIALLY, web-recent re-run) [re-run].** The quote appears in NEITHER 2017 paper. It is from **Steele, *Exploring Writing Systems and Practices in the Bronze Age Aegean*, Oxbow — cite as Steele 2024** (imprint "Published… in 2024"; LCCN year 2023 explains the 2023/2024 wobble), **p. 8**, ending "…palaeographical **arguments for further correspondences**". Also: "How do we know that Linear B adopted Linear A syllabographic sign values with little change?" is a **section** within ch. 1 (starting p. 5), not the chapter title — ch. 1 is "Exploring script adoption". The 2017 chapter (p. 98) instead says "Eleven signs can be identified with a high degree of certainty" (a, i, da, na, pa, po, ro, sa, se, ti, to; Table 6.2); the companion paper (MS p. 3) says "11 cognate signs with shared value with confidence".
2. **Younger toponym list: drop wi-na-du, fix sign-forms (PARTIALLY, web-recent re-run) [re-run].** Younger's Place Names section has PA-I-TO, SE-TO-I-JA, SU-KI-RI-TA, DI-KI-TE, TU-RI-SA, I-DA?, and KU-\*79-NI "perhaps Linear B ku-do-ni-ja". But **wi-na-du must be dropped from any Younger-attributed list** — his KH 5 commentary explicitly rejects the Inatos equation ("WI-NA-DU is not likely to be ancient Inatos"). Use his forms and hedges: DI-KI-TE (not "di-ka-te"), TU-RI-SA (not "tu-ri-so"), KU-\*79-NI only "perhaps", I-DA carries a question mark and a non-toponymic alternative.
3. **"Wholesale reshuffling" is a misquote in both the report and the companion paper (VERIFIED with correction, web-history re-run) [re-run].** Docs² (2nd ed. 1973) p. 39 actually reads "had suffered **a wholesale reshuffling in the process**" — NOT "a wholesale reshuffling process" as the archived report and the companion paper's fn 10 render it. Quote "…a wholesale reshuffling in the process" or just "a wholesale reshuffling". (1956 1st-ed. pagination not independently checked.) Bennett's "no guarantee that the same value is assigned" is now verified at Docs² p. 37 — the words are **Bennett's own 1953 statement**, block-quoted by Ventris & Chadwick; the report's framing was exactly right.
4. **"By 2005" is companion-paper wording only — and uncited there (PARTIALLY, sm2017-pdf; caveat, web-companion).** The chapter (p. 95) says "By **now**, due to new finds and better epigraphic study… **about** 64 out of 89" (72%); "2005" appears nowhere in the chapter. The companion paper (MS p. 2) does say "By 2005… 64 out of 89… 72%", but that date carries **no supporting citation** — only the 53/89 = 60% baseline is sourced (Docs² p. 33). Do not attach "By 2005" to the chapter; flag the 64/89 figure as uncited if used.
5. **Packard ratios 2:1 / 3:1 are NOT in S&M 2017 — now properly sourced (PARTIALLY, sm2017-pdf; VERIFIED, web-history re-run) [re-run].** The chapter (p. 106) gives only: 9 random frequency-matched fictitious decipherments; Ventris values yield **5x** as many parallels with **Knossian** Linear B words; mainland names show no such effect. The re-run verified: **2:1** ("just over 2 : 1… by the Ventris values" — not "Ventris's") is **van Soesbergen's** report of Packard's within-Linear-A results; **3:1** context-adjusted is **Pope & Raison, "Linear A: Changing perspectives", Études minoennes I (BCILL 14, ed. Duhoux, 1978), 24-25** — as cited by van Soesbergen; he adds **5:1 Knossos vs 4:3 mainland** (near-parity — cite the 4:3 as strengthening the negative control). The "tenth decipherment with genuine Ventris values" is van Soesbergen's account ONLY (absent from S&M 2017). Both ratios remain secondhand relative to Packard 1974 itself.
6. **No page citation for Packard's experiment is recoverable (PARTIALLY, web-history re-run) [re-run].** The pp. 112-114 range attaches in the literature (companion MS "Packard 1974, 112-4", "112-7", "113") to Packard's **vowel/sign-frequency statistics**, not the fictitious-decipherment experiment; no accessible source cites "113-115" for it; S&M 2017 p. 106 cites "Packard (1974)" pageless (their "expected frequencies" quote is Packard 1974, 82). The 112-114 vs 113-115 variance cannot be adjudicated from the open web — consult the physical 1974 monograph before citing an exact page for the experiment.
7. **SigLA co-author surname is Castellan, not "Castellano" (CONTRADICTED, web-recent re-run) [re-run].** sigla.phis.me footer: "© 2020- Ester Salgarella and Simon Castellan" (Inria, Univ. de Rennes). The repo's `docs/data-provenance.md` ("S. Castellan") is correct; the archived report is wrong. The SigLA transliteration-policy quote is now VERIFIED against the SigLA paper (Salgarella & Castellan 2020, Grapholinguistics proceedings, fluxus-editions.fr/gla5-salg.pdf) — but the report silently truncates the parenthesis, which continues "**, see discussion in § 2)**", and it is a *planned* feature stated in the paper, not database-UI wording.
8. **Salgarella quoted formulations still uncorroborated (PARTIALLY, repo-evidence).** "read but cannot understand" and "not unproblematic" appear nowhere in `docs/related/salgarella-2025.md` or the repo. The digest supports the substance (the "reading+ approach"; ~1,400 readable inscriptions) but not the wording — source both externally before quoting as her words.
9. **Carian externals not from S&M 2017 — now independently sourced (sm2017-pdf; VERIFIED, web-history re-run) [re-run].** The chapter (p. 105 + fn 21) supports only a/o/s/u retained (Schürr 1992; Adiego 2010). The re-run supplies: **Ray 1981** = Kadmos 20, 150-162 (Egyptian-Carian bilinguals); Kaunos Greek-Carian bilingual discovered **1996**. Soften "breakthrough": Kowalski 1975 was a little-known precursor with similar results, and Ray's values met scepticism until Schürr/Adiego in the early 1990s (Studia Carica 1993; the "Ray-Schürr-Adiego system") and the 1996 bilingual confirmed them.
10. **Seven o-signs claim needs narrower wording (PARTIALLY, repo-evidence).** Repo data corroborates **six** (do, mo, no, qo, so, wo) as absent from the homomorph inventory. **jo** is split: in the `litindex.py` LB_TRANSFER_SIGNS seed but zero LA-stream attestations and no row in `anchors.csv`. Safest wording: "six o-signs absent from the homomorph inventory; jo carried in the conventional AB inventory but unattested in the LA corpus stream". (The companion paper itself does state all seven — cite it, not repo evidence.)
11. **The "four pillars" framing is incomplete (PARTIALLY, sm2017-pdf).** The chapter defends the projection along at least **seven** lines: script descent/sign identity (§2), Cypriot stability (§3), shared names incl. toponyms (§4), internal sequence variation (§5), morphological adaptation trends (§7), Packard's statistics (§8), acrophony/ideograms (§9), capped by the context-of-adaptation argument (§10) — §10 is the chapter's "final justification" and is missing from the report.
12. **Sign2Vec venue uncorroborated (PARTIALLY, repo-evidence).** Method/target characterizations of Corazza 2022, Corazza et al. 2021, and Luo/Cao/Barzilay 2019 all match repo records — but "**PLOS ONE** 2022" and the author order "Corazza, Tamburini, Valério & Ferrara" appear nowhere in the repo; verify externally before citing.
13. **Palaima & Sikkenga phrasing: "repeatedly" overstates (VERIFIED with correction, web-recent re-run) [re-run].** Venue and all three phrases verified verbatim (MELETEMATA, Aegaeum 20, 1999, 599-608; offprint on Palaima's UT site), but each formulation occurs essentially **once**, in a specific context ("essential core" = Linear A's undeciphered core, p. 600; "conventionally transcribed" = Linear B multivalency, p. 601; "unknown or provisional" = Linear B's extra signs, p. 602). Use for conventional/provisional status, not a backward-projection "problem" framing.
14. **Owens reception: "toponym-heavy" is only half-right (PARTIALLY, web-recent re-run) [re-run].** Specialists balk chiefly at his religious-formula readings (Phaistos Disc as prayer; A-SA-SA-RA=Astarte) and the Indo-European Satem-branch thesis, not the place names. No formal published consensus rejection exists — phrase as "treated with explicit caution by individual specialists (e.g., Younger's published response)".
15. **Duhoux caveat can be upgraded (VERIFIED with note, sm2017-pdf).** The 81%/99% sequence-identity figures are verified **as reported by S&M 2017, p. 100** (secondary attribution to Duhoux 1989, 69); primary verification against Duhoux itself is still outstanding. Similarly, Davis 2014 vowel percentages (a 39.3 / i 25.7 / u 18.1 / e 14 / o 2.9) are verified only at second hand (companion MS, "Davis 2014, 240-2"); the Davis book itself remains unchecked.
16. **Docs² quotes: only p. 33 and p. 40 are cited in the chapter.** The "no guarantee" (Docs² p. 37) and "wholesale reshuffling" (p. 39) quotes are not from the chapter; both are now verified against the 1973 2nd edition itself (see item 3).
