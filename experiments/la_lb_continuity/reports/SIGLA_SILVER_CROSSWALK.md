## Method
Two designation normalizations, kept distinct:
- **strict** `norm_desig` — whitespace + case only (`"HT 24a"` / `"HT24a"` → `HT24A`). This defines the
  **identity crosswalk**.
- **lenient** `base_key` — additionally transliterates Greek face letters (`Α→A`, `Γ→G`…), strips
  `<> . [ ]`, takes the first member of a `+`-join, and drops a trailing 1–2-letter face after a digit
  (`HTWA1019Α → HTWA1019`, `HT24A → HT24`). Used **only** to measure how much residual divergence is
  face/join/cruft *granularity* vs genuine absence. It deliberately over-merges and is **never** an
  identity.

## Sign-identity crosswalks
- **AB## ↔ phonetic value** — recovered as a *labelled aid* by 1:1 alignment on the 309 matched
  pure-syllabic docs: **74** AB signs, **45** at ≥98 % single-value consistency, reproducing the
  canonical Linear B syllabary (AB81=KU, AB80=MA, AB53=RI, …). **This table is LEVEL_3-adjacent and is
  NOT used by the continuity primary model** — it exists to show the sources agree on shared AB signs.
- **A### ↔ silver `*###`** — **49 of 298** SigLA A-sign numbers appear as a `*###` token in silver.
  The other ~249 are SigLA composite/ligature A-signs that silver **decomposes** into syllabograms (so
  they carry no `*###` token) — the core segmentation difference (see delta report).
- **designation ↔ GORILA** — both key on GORILA designations; the join/face convention is the only
  systematic offset.

## Caveats
- The AB→value aid is learned *from silver*, so it cannot be used to "validate" silver against SigLA
  without circularity; it is presented only as a concordance indicator and firewalled from the model.
- `base_key` may over-merge genuinely distinct faces; the "genuinely absent" figures are therefore a
  **lower bound** on true corpus-composition divergence, not an exact count.
