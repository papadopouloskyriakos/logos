# INDEPENDENT_DRIFT_EVIDENCE_AUDIT — §VIII

Audit of whether the drift operations the known pairs exhibit can be grounded in evidence INDEPENDENT
of those pairs. (Operations are OBSERVED for feasibility only; never used to fit a model.)

| operation | example (quarantined) | independent support | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| vowel u→i | TU-RU-SA → tu-ri-so (RU→RI) | WEAK | not an independently-attested spelling rule; clear example is a known pair → circular |
| vowel a→o | TU-RU-SA → tu-ri-so (SA→SO) | WEAK | genuine linguistic change, not an orthographic variant |
| derivational suffix (+i-jo, +de) | I-DA→i-da-i-jo, DI-KI-TA→di-ka-ta-de | MODERATE | suffixes are real LB morphology, but bare→suffixed matching is a length change that inflates false positives |

Independent sources checked: LB-internal spelling variation, scribal variants, LB adjectival/allative
morphology, palaeographic A↔B equivalence. **None** grounds the systematic vowel-quality drift the known
pairs require without reusing the quarantined pairs themselves. The admissible independent evidence
(morphological suffixes, allographs) does not span the genuine sound-change drift.
