- **Thresholds pre-committed at `00fb9eaec78e6de8ba684263fabc5eed6a76f07f`** (before any run;
  hash recorded inside `results/power_precheck.json`, `run_head_commit` identical).

  seed 20260703. Wall-clock **51.4 s CPU**.
- **Validity:** s=0 false-fire 0.02 (bound 0.14) ✓; s=13 power 1.00 (bound 0.90) ✓.
- **Primary curve (h=10):** power = 0.02 / 0.00 / 0.04 / 0.06 / **0.26** / **0.98** / 1.00 /
  1.00 at s = 0 / 0.5 / 1 / 2 / 3 / 5 / 8 / 13 (mean top-1 at s=5: 0.496 vs chance 0.0137).
- **Verdict: MARGINAL** — GO required ≥0.80 power at s ≤ 3 (observed 0.26); MARGINAL band met
  at s=5 (0.98). Detection onset sits between s=3 and s=5, vs B.2's real-control firing at s=2
  on its easier 13-class problem — the 73-way task at n=51/h=10 needs a distinctly stronger
  signal.
- **Sensitivity (reported, non-binding):** h=20 detects best at s=3 (power 0.50 vs 0.26 at
  h=10) — at this anchor count, a larger held-out set buys detection resolution faster than the
  smaller train set costs accuracy. Design guidance for the final prereg, not a threshold
  change.
- **Escalation (pre-committed MARGINAL branch):** the operator decides whether Phase 1
  proceeds. The curves say: a real signal of moderate strength (s≈5, i.e. ~5× unit noise in
  embedding space) is detectable with near-certainty at the actual design size; a weak one
  (s≤3) mostly is not. Options the curves support: (a) proceed with h=20 and accept the weak-
  signal blind spot as a disclosed limitation; (b) gate Phase 1 on acquiring Steele & Meißner
  2017 first (the Cypriot-stable axis both sharpens the split and adds anchors' independent
  corroboration); (c) stop here and record the design bound.
