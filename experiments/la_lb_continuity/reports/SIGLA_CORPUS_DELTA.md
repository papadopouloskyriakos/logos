## Only-in-one-source (the largest delta)
Silver holds **730** inscriptions genuinely absent from SigLA — dominated by roundel/nodule
enumeration (`HTWA…` ×524) and inscribed objects/libation material (`…ZA/ZB/ZF/ZG…`, e.g. `APZA1`,
`ARKHZC8`, `CRZF5`, `IOZA…`). SigLA holds **143** genuinely absent from silver. Neither corpus is a
superset. **Consequence:** silver stays the LA transcription baseline (larger, and the frozen slot
classifier runs on it); SigLA can *extend* coverage for its 143 exclusive documents and supplies the
sign-identity/palaeography layer.

## Segmentation / transcription differences (210 / 575)
Sign-count delta (SigLA − silver), clamped ±3: `{-3:6, -2:6, -1:48, 0:365, +1:57, +2:31, +3:62}`.
The asymmetry (more SigLA-heavy) reflects SigLA counting logograms/numerals/composite glyphs that
silver folds or omits; the silver-heavy tail reflects silver **decomposing composite signs** into
syllabograms. Example (silver-more): `HT24A` SigLA 17 vs silver 23 (SigLA keeps 6 A-series composites
that silver decomposes). On the 575 matched docs, SigLA glyphs split **AB 3331 / A-series 948** — the
A-series fraction is where the two transcription philosophies diverge.

## Sign-ID disagreements
At AB level the sources agree (learned map reproduces the syllabary). The disagreement is categorical:
**~249 of 298 SigLA A-sign numbers have no `*###` counterpart in silver** because silver renders those
graphs as decomposed syllabograms rather than as A-series composite IDs. This is the
palaeographic-vs-normalized split — recorded, not resolved.

## Damaged-sign representation (structural difference)
- **SigLA:** per-glyph — `sign=null` (unidentified) or `is_divider=true`; no graded restoration flag.
- **Silver:** carries `*###` for undeciphered graphs and a `stream` with `div`/`nl` tokens; restored
  signs are marked in the source transliteration conventions.
The two encode uncertainty differently; a unified uncertainty layer is **deferred to §V/§VII** and must
be handled explicitly (transcription-variant sensitivity), not silently normalized.

## Metadata disagreements (on the 575 matched)
- **Site (29):** romanization/typos, not real relocation — SigLA `Mallia` vs silver `Malia`; SigLA
  `Haghios Stephanos` vs silver misspelled `Stehanos`; and an ambiguous `ARKH` prefix (SigLA
  `Arkhanes` vs silver `Arkhalkhori`/Arkalochori) — **flagged for adjudication in §VIII**, since site
  is a slot-classifier feature.
- **Period (14 diff, 145 n/a):** granularity/notation — e.g. `ARKH2` SigLA `LM I` vs silver `LMIB`;
  `KH100` `LM IB` vs `LMIIIA`; `PH10` `MM IIB` vs `MMII`. 145 not comparable (one side blank).
- **Support (87):** object-type nomenclature boundaries differ (Nodule/Sealing/Roundel).

## Net assessment
Deterministic reconciliation is achievable and **complete enough for use**: a strict identity crosswalk
(575) plus a characterized residual. No blocking licensing/determinism issue. The site-name ambiguities
(esp. `ARKH`) and the transcription-variant/uncertainty encoding must be carried forward explicitly into
the candidate freeze (§VII) and target audit (§VIII), because both feed slot features and the toponym
comparison.
