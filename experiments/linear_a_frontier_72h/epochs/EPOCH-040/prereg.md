# EPOCH-040 — INTRA-WORD SIGN REPETITION (reduplication vs anti-repetition; L2/L3)

## QUESTION
Do Linear A words contain REPEATED signs (the same sign appearing >=2 times within a
single word) MORE than chance (reduplication, a morphological process) or LESS than
chance (anti-repetition, a phonotactic/orthographic constraint)? Is the direction
ROBUST across independent SITES?

Pure distributional structure (L2/L3): signs are ANONYMOUS tokens. No phonetics, no
sound, no meaning, no reading. "Repetition" is a distributional statistic, NOT a claim
about sound or morpheme meaning.

## DATA
- corpus/silver/inscriptions_structured.json — word tokens with `t=='word'` and a
  `signs` list. Use words of length >= 2 (a repeat is only possible at len>=2).
- Linear B (DAMOS) via `load_b_damos()[0]` — POSITIVE-CONTROL benchmark ONLY.

## FROZEN METRIC
`rep_rate` = fraction of words (len>=2) containing at least one repeated sign (any
sign appearing >=2 times within the word).

## FROZEN NULL (i.i.d.-unigram, LENGTH-MATCHED)
For each observed word of length L, draw L signs i.i.d. from the GLOBAL unigram
frequency distribution (over all len>=2 word tokens). Compute `rep_rate` over the
synthetic corpus. >=2000 draws. TWO-SIDED p for "observed rep_rate deviates from null".
Report direction: excess = reduplication; deficit = avoidance.

This tests whether words repeat signs more/less than expected from sign frequencies
+ word lengths alone.

## PROTOCOL (in order)
0. Inspect: n words len>=2; observed rep_rate; length distribution; per-site counts.
1. FREEZE prereg + plan_hash + machinery.py (with __main__ self-check).
2. GLOBAL: observed rep_rate; i.i.d.-unigram null mean + two-sided p + direction.
3. POSITIVE CONTROL FIRST (gates verdict). On LB:
   (a) DETECT — plant a known repetition EXCESS (inject reduplicated words) and
       confirm the null detects excess (p<=0.05, correct direction); AND plant a
       known DEFICIT (remove all repeat-words) and confirm it detects deficit.
   (b) FALSE-POSITIVE — on words generated BY the i.i.d.-unigram model itself
       (true H0), rejection rate <=0.10 across >=20 sets.
   Failure of either => MACHINERY_UNINFORMATIVE.
4. LA MAIN — CROSS-SITE held-out: per site with >=50 words, compute rep_rate + null
   p + direction; count sites significant same direction; require CONSISTENT
   direction. Leave-one-site-out on Haghia Triada (the largest site).
5. FROZEN MECHANICAL VERDICT (count):
   - REDUPLICATION_EXCESS_CROSS_SITE iff PC passed AND global EXCESS significant
     (p<=0.05) AND >=3 sites significant excess AND survives leave-one-site-out.
   - REPETITION_AVOIDANCE_CROSS_SITE iff PC passed AND global DEFICIT significant
     (p<=0.05) AND >=3 sites significant deficit AND survives leave-one-site-out.
   - REPETITION_AT_CHANCE iff global p>0.05 (rep_rate consistent with i.i.d.).
   - REPETITION_SITE_LOCAL iff global significant BUT <3 sites OR direction flips
     OR collapses under LOO.
   - REPETITION_UNDERPOWERED iff <3 sites have >=50 words.
   - MACHINERY_UNINFORMATIVE iff PC failed.
6. WRITE OUTPUTS to the PATH CONTRACT paths.

## NON-CIRCULARITY
Signs carry NO phonetic/sound/meaning/reading. L2/L3 statistics ONLY. LB is a
positive-control benchmark ONLY (never the target of the claim). Verdict is
MECHANICAL, computed from the frozen rule above — proposer/operator, never
adjudicator.

## SEEDS / DRAWS
N_DRAWS=2000 (global, per-site, LOO); PC_DETECT_DRAWS=2000; PC_FP_DRAWS=1000;
N_FP_SETS=20. Seeds fixed in machinery.py.
